Hello!

My name is Cristina, and I work at EcoQuants, where I manage, prepare, visualize, and conduct analyses with environmental data.

At EcoQuants, I have contributed to a variety of projects and dashboards, including the Marine Biodiversity Observation Network (MBON) Pole-to-Pole Project and the U.S. Department of Energy Marine Energy Toolkit. I have also conducted some of my own personal projects, including a Shiny app visualizing intertidal temperature trends in the Santa Barbara Channel of California and a Shiny app displaying disparities in U.S. food security across several variables.

I am a creative person at heart and deeply enjoy the process of developing innovative and engaging methods of data communication. In my work, I strive to balance my creative interests with technical expertise and ethical concerns, and thus am fascinated by the power of data science and computer programming in working toward environmental goals. In the future, I hope to continue to deepen my data science skills for the purpose of comprehending, communicating, and developing solutions to complex environmental problems.

When I’m not coding or working on environmental projects, I like to spend time in nature, play guitar, and listen to music.

Projects

Marine Energy Toolkit Shiny App · EcoQuants; U.S. Department of Energy
A tool for developers and regulators to fully understand the location, scope, environmental effects, and regulations and management procedures of marine energy projects in the United States. Includes a Shiny app to view data and generate reports.

Marine Intertidal Temperatures Shiny App · Hofmann Lab @ UCSB
A Shiny app that uses maps and timeseries plots to allow users to interactively visualize intertidal temperature data collected by biomimetic loggers along the coast of the northeastern Pacific Ocean.

U.S. Food Security Shiny App · Food, Agriculture, and the Environment Course @ UCSB
A class research project that provides background information on U.S. food security and food access, allows users to visualize and understand disparities in food security in the U.S. via interactive maps and plots, and presents solutions that researchers and activists have proposed to combat these disparities, inequalities, and injustices.

Pedestrianization GIS Project · Environmental Applications of GIS Course @ UCSB
A class research project that used ArcGIS Pro to determine how much area in Los Angeles County is devoted to automotive transportation and which areas in the Los Angeles Basin provide the greatest opportunity for urban planning toward pedestrianization.

Music Listening Data Shiny App · Environmental Applications of GIS Course @ UCSB
I am currently in the process of creating a dashboard mimicking the UI of last.fm to interactively display the entirety of my personal music streaming data, which I obtained directly from Spotify. This is purely an endeavor of personal interest and scope, but I am excited to share it with anyone who might be interested when it is complete. ･ﾟ✧*:･
